What this tab shows

Headline discrimination (AUC) and calibration (Brier) for the unified 148-phage × 369-bacteria panel, reported separately along two axes — held-out bacteria (cold-start host) and held-out phages (cold-start phage). Bars (lollipops) are per-fold AUC values; dots are the aggregate.

How to read it

  • AUC is the probability a random true-positive pair is ranked above a random true-negative pair. 0.5 is chance; 1.0 is perfect ordering. CIs come from 1,000 bootstrap resamples of the eval bacteria (bacteria-axis) or the eval phages (phage-axis).
  • Brier is mean squared error of predicted probability vs 0/1 label. Lower is better. ~0.18 corresponds to a model that's pretty good at ranking but systematically overconfident — see the Calibration tab.
  • Why phage-axis AUC > bacteria-axis AUC by ~8 pp: phage-axis folds hold out entire phages but keep all 369 bacteria in training (so each test pair has a host with rich label coverage). Bacteria-axis folds remove host-side signal. The gap is structural training-data coverage, not a quality signal.

Caveats

  • Guelin dominates the panel (96.6% of pairs). Aggregate AUC is Guelin-weighted — see Cross-source for the BASEL-specific breakdown and deficit.
  • Per-phage blending is retired under CHISEL; any +2 pp gain from it was partly a leakage artifact pre-CH02 and is not deployable for unseen phages.

What am I looking at here?

What this tab shows

Reliability diagrams: for each predicted-probability decile (0-10%, 10-20%, …), what fraction of pairs actually lysed? A perfectly calibrated model would put every dot exactly on the diagonal y = x. Dots above the diagonal mean the model underpredicts lysis in that bin; below means it overpredicts.

ECE (Expected Calibration Error, in the table) is the weighted mean of |observed − predicted| across deciles. Lower is better. Target is <0.02 after the CH09 isotonic calibrator; the raw uncalibrated Guelin model sits around 0.12.

How to read it

  • Raw curves (solid) show the out-of-the-box LightGBM probabilities. Expect heavy overprediction in deciles 6-9 — the CHISEL training label is "lysed at some concentration" which is more permissive than the deployment question "does it lyse at THIS concentration?"
  • LOOF-isotonic (Guelin, dashed) applies a 10-fold leave-one-fold-out isotonic regression. Nearly collapses onto the diagonal for Guelin (ECE ≈ 0.005).
  • Transferred-isotonic (BASEL, dashed) takes the Guelin-fit calibrator and applies it to BASEL. It partially helps (64% bacteria-axis / 45% phage-axis ECE closure) but leaves a substantial residual — confirming the remaining BASEL miscalibration is a feature-space (TL17-bias) problem, not a threshold problem.

Caveats

  • Deciles with very few pairs (n < 50) have wide Clopper-Pearson CIs. The observed fraction can wobble a lot. ECE weights by bin count so it already accounts for this.
  • The CH09 calibrator is the production remedy for Guelin overconfidence. Applying it is a 5-line post-processing step at inference (see ch09_calibrator.pkl).

Top-30 LightGBM feature importance

Averaged across 30 fits (10 folds × 3 seeds) per axis. Colors map to the feature slot; hover for the full name and the number of folds that retained the feature after RFE (n_folds_selected / 30).

What am I looking at here?

What this tab shows

LightGBM's split-gain importance averaged across 30 model fits (10 CV folds × 3 random seeds) for the chosen axis. A feature's importance is the total gain across all tree splits it was chosen for, normalized so the top-30 are comparable. Bar color encodes the feature slot (host_surface, phage_projection, pair_depo_capsule, etc.).

How to read it

  • The n_folds_selected / 30 tooltip tells you how robust the importance is: a feature retained by RFE in all 30 fits is much more trustworthy than one kept in only 8/30.
  • pair_concentration__log10_pfu_ml sits high (~#4). That's expected — it's the one feature that directly tells the model "which rung of the titration are we evaluating?"
  • pair_depo_capsule × in_cluster_N features dominate the pair-level block (22% of total importance). This is the Gate 1 "depolymerase-can-degrade-this-capsule" mechanism validated in GT03 — the only pairwise feature family that cleanly lifts AUC.

Caveats

  • Split-gain importance is not a causal-effect estimate. It measures how much the feature helped the tree structure, not how much it would help if wiggled on a real pair (that's SHAP — see Pair explorer).
  • Correlated features split the importance. If host_serotype and host_o_type are redundant, LightGBM arbitrarily picks one per split and the importance goes to whichever. Don't infer "one doesn't matter" from a single axis.
  • Defense features are intentionally down-weighted by RFE because of lineage confounding — a defense-system k-mer that correlates with phylogroup gets pruned, not because it lacks biological signal, but because the signal is absorbed by host_typing.

What am I looking at here?

What this tab shows

A rollup of the Feature importance tab, grouped by slot (the logical feature block a feature comes from). Each card shows the slot's feature count and the sum of its features' importances. A slot that contributes 25% of total importance has a meaningful signal; a slot that contributes <2% is either noise or redundant with another slot.

How to read it

  • pair_depo_capsule (~22%): the Gate 1 adsorption mechanism. Phage has depolymerase for host's capsule type.
  • host_surface, host_typing: host-side features including capsule cluster membership, serotype, phylogroup. These are where most host-side signal lives.
  • phage_projection (Arm 3 Moriniere per-receptor-class k-mer fractions, 13 dim): panel-independent receptor identity signal. Replaces the old Guelin-derived TL17 projection slot.
  • pair_receptor_omp: the Gate 2 mechanism (receptor × OMP) — contributes little because host OMP HMM scores are nearly constant (CV 0.01-0.17) across E. coli.
  • host_defense: Gate 3. Contributes some AUC but regresses top-3 ranking due to lineage confounding (defense subtypes correlate with phylogroup).

Caveats

  • The pair-level blocks aren't in the canonical SLOT_SPECS — they're cross-terms built at feature-assembly time. Labels end with __cross or __in_cluster_N.
  • A slot can have high aggregate importance but be dominated by 1-2 highly-ranked features (e.g. concentration sits inside pair_concentration and eclipses the slot's others).

What am I looking at here?

What this tab shows

A SHAP waterfall for one specific (bacterium, phage) pair. The model's predicted logit for this pair is decomposed into per-feature contributions summing to the final prediction:

predicted logit = base value + Σ SHAPfeature

The base value is the model's average log-odds prediction across the whole training set (reflects the base rate of lysis). Each bar then shows how much one feature's value for THIS pair pushed the prediction away from base, in log-odds units. The header at the top shows base + Σ SHAP = logit and that arithmetic should be exact (TreeExplainer guarantee).

How to read it

  • Red bars push toward "no lysis" (more negative logit). Blue bars push toward "lysis" (more positive logit).
  • The sign and magnitude are about this feature's value on this pair, given the other features — not about whether the feature is "good" or "bad" in general. A feature with value X might contribute +0.5 on one pair and −0.8 on another if tree paths branch differently.
  • To convert logit → probability: p = 1 / (1 + exp(-logit)). logit −6.6 ≈ 0.14%, 0 = 50%, +3 ≈ 95%.
  • Only the top 20 features by |SHAP| are shown, but the prediction-logit header accounts for all features' contributions.

Why pairs look so different from each other

  • Feature values differ. Every pair has its own host genome (capsule, serotype, defense repertoire) and phage genome (length, GC, receptor fractions). Broad-host Straboviridae phages contribute very different SHAPs than narrow-host Autographiviridae.
  • LightGBM learns interactions. SHAP attribution is path-dependent — the same feature value can flip contribution sign depending on which tree branches were taken (driven by the other features' values).

Does it make biological sense?

  • Directionally yes, with caveats: capsule-cluster features dominating the host side is the GT03-validated Gate 1 mechanism. Concentration always contributing positively for lysis at high titer makes sense.
  • Be skeptical of individual feature SHAPs as causal claims — LightGBM can recover statistical signal that reflects confounding (lineage, panel size, label noise) rather than mechanism. SHAPs are best interpreted at the slot level.

How to read it

  • Label is the binary ground truth (1 = lysed, 0 = not) at the pair's max-concentration observation. For Guelin that's neat (log10_pfu_ml ≈ 8.7), for BASEL it's 9.0.

Good rows to look at

  • Top of "|p − label| desc" with predicted < 0.1, label = 1: missed narrow-host positives. NILS53 × narrow-host phages typically show up here — broad-phage priors suppress the narrow-host signal.
  • Filter to source = basel to see the BASEL bacteria-axis deficit concretely: pairs where the model assigns broad-host-phage probabilities that don't match BASEL's narrower actual ranges.

Caveats

  • ~10% of rows have score='n' upstream — these are labeled 0 but the experimental result was genuinely ambiguous. An apparent false-positive might be a labeling artifact.
  • The max-concentration evaluation convention means a pair that lyses only at neat dilution counts as a positive. Pairs whose only positive replicate is at neat are not filtered (CH10 reverted that).

What am I looking at here?

What this tab shows

The same per-pair SHAP decomposition as the Pair explorer, but aggregated at the slot level. Each bar is the sum of SHAP contributions from every feature in that slot.

How to read it

  • Bars read the same way as the Pair explorer: red = push toward "no lysis", blue = push toward "lysis". Here it's the slot's net direction.
  • The slot label shows feature count in parentheses — e.g. pair_depo_capsule (8) means the bar sums 8 features inside that slot.
  • Compare to the Pair explorer: if individual pair_depo_capsule__in_cluster_N features there look noisy or have opposing signs, but the slot total here is cleanly negative, trust the slot total — that's the Gate 1 adsorption signal.

Why slot-level is more trustworthy

Correlated features (e.g. in_cluster_3 and in_cluster_6 on phages with similar depolymerase repertoires) split LightGBM's attribution depending on which branch it split first. Per-feature SHAPs can flip sign between similar pairs on the same feature, but slot sums stay stable. See Glossary → Slot for the full explanation.

What am I looking at here?

What this tab shows

For each cohort (e.g. all Guelin pairs), the mean per-pair SHAP for every feature, summed per slot. Bars group by slot; each color is a cohort. Same units as the Pair explorer: log-odds push per slot, positive = toward lysis.

How to read it

  • Direction & magnitude match the Pair-level semantics, but averaged across the cohort. A slot that's always noisy and sign-flips pair-by-pair averages toward 0; a slot that systematically pushes one way shows up here.
  • Different-magnitude bars for the same slot across cohorts = systematic differential. For example, if phage_projection averages +0.5 on Guelin but −0.1 on BASEL, the model is leaning on phage-side signal much more for Guelin pairs.
  • Group by label to see what the model activates on for positives vs negatives. Clean separation = model is well-differentiated; overlapping bars = model is predicting close to base rate for that slot.

Caveats

  • Cohort sizes differ widely (Guelin 35k, BASEL 1.2k). Small-cohort bars have more sampling noise.
  • This is a snapshot-level aggregate, not a causal-effect estimate. Don't read "phage_projection is bad on BASEL" as "remove phage_projection"; it means "TL17-basis features behave differently on BASEL pairs," which is the load-bearing TL17-bias finding already documented in chisel-unified-kfold-baseline.
